# Part 2 — Glints MY (honest status)

**Glints search works perfectly** (see `VALIDATION.md`): the GraphQL `searchJobsV3` endpoint returns
real MY listings *with structured salary* (e.g. `4000-10000 MYR /MONTH`) — better salary data than
JobStreet, which only gives text.

**Glints JD *detail* extraction is anti-bot-sensitive.** The full JD lives in the job page's
`__NEXT_DATA__` (Draft.js blocks) or a JSON-LD `JobPosting`. I successfully pulled one full Glints JD
during testing — **"Presales Engineer" @ ICT Distribution, 6,245 chars** — but after a burst of rapid
requests Glints started returning a minimal HTML shell (rate-limiting / bot detection). I stopped
rather than hammer it.

**What this means for the build (a real, useful finding):**
- Glints needs **gentle fetching**: throttle + jitter between detail requests, a realistic browser
  User-Agent, and backoff/retry — or a proper session. This is exactly why discovery belongs in a
  swappable `Provider` with its own rate-limit policy (see `ARCHITECTURE.md` §7).
- JobStreet detail (app-state parse) was **robust and un-throttled** across all requests — so for
  Malaysia, **JobStreet is the reliable primary; Glints is a strong secondary** (great for salary),
  fetched more carefully.

*(Re-runnable later once the rate limit resets — the extractor is written; it just needs pacing.)*
